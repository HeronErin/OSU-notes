## Differentials

The differential of f at point x=a coresponds to a change in x, dx is delta x

$$ df = f'(a)dx $$

Idea:

$$ L(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x-a) $$
Suppose x change from x=a to a+dx
$$ \Delta f = f(a+dx) - f(a)$$
$$ \Delta f ~=L_a(a+dx)-f(a) $$
$$ \Delta f ~= f'(a)dt$$
$$ \Delta f ~= df $$
